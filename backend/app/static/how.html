How it works

Modeled on the memory you already have.

Humans don't keep one bucket of memories. We live through events, consolidate the useful ones in our sleep, and act from distilled habits. Mnemo copies that flow — with Qwen3.7-Max doing the dreaming.

The loop

Experience → Consolidate → Decide.

01 · Experience

Every outcome becomes an episode

Each routed ticket and its confirmed or corrected outcome is written as a raw, timestamped episode — the immutable source of truth, embedded with Qwen text-embedding-v4 and indexed in pgvector.

02 · Consolidate

The Dreaming loop

On a schedule, Qwen3.7-Max reflects over episodic history and distils durable knowledge upward: semantic facts and procedural IF→THEN rules, each carrying provenance back to the episodes that earned it.

03 · Decide

Budgeted retrieval

New tickets retrieve a finite working set — a few high-confidence rules, a fact, the closest precedents — re-ranked by similarity, recency, importance, and confidence. Every decision cites what fired.

Inside the Dreaming loop

Consolidation is not an INSERT statement.

Credit assignment

Rules that drive correct decisions gain confidence; rules that fire wrongly lose it, and below a threshold they deactivate. Trust tracks reality.

Conflict resolution

When new evidence contradicts a rule — the org changed a policy — the refined rule supersedes the stale one, keeping a lineage link instead of piling up duplicates.

Recency-weighted evidence

Recent experience outweighs stale experience, and corrected mistakes count double. This is what lets unlearning converge in sessions, not months.

Decay

Memories that go unused fade in retrieval weight. The store stays sharp instead of growing into a junk drawer.
